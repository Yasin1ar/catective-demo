After twenty years in the business, you start to notice patterns. The same mistakes. The same blind spots. Here's what I've learned:

**1. Write everything down.** Memory is a liar. Timestamps, names, quotes—get them on paper. You'll thank yourself at 3 AM when the pieces finally fit.

**2. Assume nothing.** The "obvious" lead is wrong half the time. Verify. Cross-reference. Follow the thread even when it seems too simple.

**3. Talk to the janitor.** The people nobody notices see everything. The night shift. The interns. The person who restocks the break room. They have stories.

**4. Sleep on it.** Your subconscious is better at puzzles than you are. Stuck? Walk away. Come back tomorrow. The answer will often be waiting.

**5. Know when to close the file.** Some cases don't have endings. Some questions don't have answers. Knowing the difference saves you years of chasing ghosts.

That's it. No magic. Just process. Now go find something.
