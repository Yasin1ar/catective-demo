The call came at three in the morning. *When don't they?* A collector's prized first edition of *The Maltese Falcon* had vanished from a climate-controlled safe room. The door was still locked from the inside. The window hadn't been opened in years.

I've learned that impossible crimes usually have very possible explanations. Someone had a key. Someone knew the code. The question was always *who*—and *why*.

By dawn, I'd ruled out the butler. *He was in Argentina.* The real culprit turned out to be considerably more mundane: a humidity sensor gone bad, a panic-stricken conservator, and a manuscript hastily relocated to "safety" in the basement. Case closed. The book was returned. The collector offered me a cigar. I declined—I don't smoke.

**Lessons learned:** Always check the maintenance logs. And never trust a locked room. Locks keep honest people honest. That's about it.
